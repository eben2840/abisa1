Quick Aims:

  • There is a strong belief that the institutionalization of people is done because they are no longer able to carry out activities that they used to enjoy, or to carry out their own routine activities of daily living that deprives people of maintaining their independence, and effectively de-motivates and increasingly promotes low self-esteem.

ABISA Support:

Registered with the Care Quality Commission as a service delivering care and support to individuals residing in their own homes, or their families homes within the community. Our mission is to offer excellent services tailored to an individualâ€™s needs.

We aim to foster independence in the people we care for, supporting them to think critically and act autonomously. We promote the concept of taking measured risks within an environment that empowers people to accomplish as much as they can on their own, with just the right level of support.

What we offer:

ABISA Support boasts a team of experienced professionals, including a registered manager and trained specialized support staff well-versed in mental health, autism, and learning disabilities. All staff undergo comprehensive training to meet diverse service user needs, working collaboratively within a multi-disciplinary care approach. We prioritise effective staff development programs. Our recruitment selects individuals who share our values and contribute to a homely environment for service users Adequate resources ensure effective staff training. We promote healthy lifestyles, encouraging regular exercise, sleep, and proper nutrition.
