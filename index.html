What we offer

Please contact Austin Amorighoye, Referrals Manager, to discuss any clients or to make a referral. We can then arrange a comprehensive assessment by qualified staff. You will be kept updated every step of the way.

We typically receive referrals from hospitals, private individuals, secure units, and local authorities.

Want to make a   Referral

Clinical Commissioning Groups and Local Authorities are enabled to transfer the management and delivery of health and social care packages, with varying levels of complexity, to ABISA support in their role as a supported living service provider. This reduces cost, minimises delays, and promotes a pathway that supports the service user.

Service users’ benefit from increased choice, control, and independence in their daily lives through the provision of trained support workers. Assistance can be provided with all aspects of daily life including the utilisation of personal budgets, arranging transport. Each package is discussed and tailored to the individual.

All Amazing   Benefits

ABISA Support offers an innovative blend of traditional and modern care approaches, creating a holistic environment that empowers service users to achieve their goals. Our main aim is to assist people to transition from psychiatric hospitals, medium secure units, or residential care homes to live in the community.

Our personalised, targeted support prioritises the well-being of service users, encouraging them to embrace reasonable risk-taking while upholding their privacy, dignity, independence, choice, rights, and fulfilment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Service users will be supported in their own homes, family home, or supported living accommodation with their own tenancy.

The referring authority will have responsibility for the cost of care and support services. Payments can be made directly, or service user may make payments to ABISA Limited through their individual budget, by direct payment or private funding.

ABISA Support is dedicated to delivering safe, high-quality, person-centered support, with a clear governance structure meeting legal responsibilities, best practices, and engaging with staff, service users, and stakeholders.

Abisa Support

Abisa Support will provide support to people from 18-65 years, including physically disability, people with mental health illness, autism, learning disabilities and people with drugs and alcohol problems.
